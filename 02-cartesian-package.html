The Cartesian Package

Map, quiz, and sort the four pillars of Descartes' view of human nature

Pillar I

Disembodied Mind

The mind (or soul) is a distinct substance from the body. Who you really are is your thinking self — the body is incidental.

🔒
✓
Pillar II

Radical Freedom

The will is free — perhaps the only thing we possess that is genuinely unlimited. We are free from causal determination.

🔒
✓
Pillar III

Reason Over Emotion

We are fundamentally rational creatures. Emotions are disturbances that interfere with the clear perception of truth.

🔒
✓
Pillar IV

Asocial Nature

Being social is merely accidental to being human. The essential self exists independently of any social group or community.

From Descartes, Meditation II

"Thinking is another attribute of the soul; and here I discover what properly belongs to myself. This alone is inseparable from me... I am, then, in the strict sense only a thing that thinks."

From Descartes, Meditation IV

"The will is so free in its nature, that it can never be constrained... it is the will alone which I experience in myself to be so great that I can conceive no other idea to be more great."
