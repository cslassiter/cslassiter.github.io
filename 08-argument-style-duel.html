How it works

Each round shows two arguments for the same conclusion, written in opposite styles — one from each of the eleven pairs of temperaments in this unit. Just choose the one that lands better for you. There are no right answers.

After each choice, you’ll see which style you picked and why it works. At the end you’ll get a profile of the styles you gravitate toward as a reader — your argumentative taste.

Why “no right answer” is the whole point. Which style is best depends on the goal of the exchange (Walton & Krabbe, Commitment in Dialogue, 1995). A tight deductive chain is perfect for a proof and wrong for a eulogy. So the duel isn’t testing whether you can spot the “good” argument — it’s surfacing the moves you instinctively trust, so you can notice them and, later, borrow the others.

Two pilot formats. The quick duel shows one round per pair (11 rounds) and reshuffles which argument you see when you retake it. The full duel runs both rounds of all eleven pairs (22 rounds) for a sharper, more reliable read.
